Actionable GEO: 10 Strategies to Get Your Content Cited by AI

In the new landscape of Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO), the goal is not just to rank, but to be referenced. Getting your content cited as a source in an AI-generated answer requires a strategic approach focused on clarity, authority, and AI-friendliness. Here are 10 actionable strategies you can implement today.

Strategy 1: Solidify Your Technical Foundation (Especially on Bing)

Before an AI can cite your content, it must be able to find and understand it. Since many LLMs, including ChatGPT, use Bing for their live web searches, optimising for Bing is no longer optional. Ensure your site is properly indexed, has a clean XML sitemap, and is free of crawl errors. A solid technical SEO foundation is the price of entry.

Strategy 2: Structure for Clarity: Why Headings, Lists, and FAQs are Crucial

AI models favour content that is well-organized and direct. They need to parse information quickly and efficiently. Use a logical hierarchy of headings (H2, H3) to structure your articles. Employ bullet points and numbered lists to break down complex information. A dedicated FAQ section that directly answers specific questions is particularly powerful, as it provides clear, extractable answer snippets.

Best Practices for AI-Friendly Structure:

  • Use descriptive heading hierarchy (H2, H3, H4)
  • Break complex information into bullet points
  • Create dedicated FAQ sections
  • Use numbered lists for step-by-step processes
  • Include summary boxes for key takeaways

Strategy 3: Enrich Your Content with Specific Data and Statistics

AI loves verifiable facts. Content that includes specific data points, statistics, and figures is often perceived as more authoritative and is highly citable. When you make a claim, back it up with a number. For example, instead of saying "user queries are longer," say "user queries are now longer, averaging 23 words." This makes your content a prime source for citation.

Pro Tip: Always include the source and date of your statistics. AI systems value transparency and recency in data sources.

Strategy 4: Leverage Firsthand Experience with Case Studies and Reviews

Originality and firsthand experience are powerful differentiators. AI is designed to synthesize existing information, so content that provides a unique perspective through case studies, personal reviews, or proprietary research is incredibly valuable. This type of content offers information that the AI cannot find elsewhere, making it a highly attractive source.

Types of Original Content That AI Values:

  • Case Studies: Real-world examples with specific results
  • Original Research: Surveys, studies, or proprietary data
  • Personal Reviews: Hands-on experience with products or services
  • Industry Insights: Behind-the-scenes knowledge from your expertise

Strategy 5: Write for AI Readability: Clarity and Quotable Snippets Win

Clarity trumps everything. Your content needs to be AI-friendly, which means using clear, unambiguous language. Avoid jargon where possible, and define it when necessary. Think in terms of "quotable snippets." Write concise, self-contained sentences and paragraphs that can be easily lifted and inserted into a generated answer. Phrases like "In summary," or "The key takeaway is..." can also help signal important information to LLM crawlers.

Strategy 6: Master Question-Based Queries

Users interact with LLMs conversationally, primarily by asking questions. Your content strategy should reflect this. Optimise your articles around specific questions your audience is asking. Frame your headings and sections as questions (e.g., "How Does GEO Differ from SEO?") rather than just keywords ("GEO vs. SEO"). This directly aligns your content with the structure of a user's prompt.

Question-Based Content Framework:

  1. Identify Core Questions: What are your audience's main concerns?
  2. Structure Content as Answers: Each section should answer a specific question
  3. Use Conversational Headings: "How to..." "What is..." "Why does..."
  4. Provide Complete Answers: Don't leave questions half-answered

Strategy 7: Showcase Authority with Credentials and Citations

Trust is a critical factor for inclusion. An AI is more likely to cite a source that appears credible. You can signal this by:

  • Including author credentials: Showcase the author's expertise and experience.
  • Citing authoritative sources: Link out to respected industry publications, academic papers, and official reports to back up your claims. This shows that your content is well-researched and part of a larger conversation.

Building Content Authority:

  • Display author bio and credentials prominently
  • Link to authoritative external sources
  • Include publication dates and update timestamps
  • Use schema markup for author and organization information
  • Showcase relevant certifications or awards

Strategy 8: Optimize Your Multimedia with Alt Text and Captions

AI Overviews and other generative experiences are increasingly visual. Including relevant, high-quality images, infographics, and videos makes your content more engaging and useful. Crucially, you must optimise this media with descriptive alt text and captions. This provides another layer of context for AI systems to understand and potentially feature your visuals alongside your text.

Visual Content Optimization Checklist:

  • Write descriptive, keyword-rich alt text
  • Add captions that provide context
  • Use structured data for images
  • Optimize file names with relevant keywords
  • Include transcripts for video content

Strategy 9: Use Summaries and Bullet Points to Your Advantage

LLM crawlers are designed to extract the essence of a document quickly. Summaries and bulleted lists are perfectly formatted for this task. Begin your article with a concise summary of what the reader will learn. Use bullet points not just for lists, but to outline key benefits, features, or steps. This scannable format is favoured by both human readers and AI crawlers.

Key Takeaway: Structured content with clear summaries and bullet points is 3x more likely to be cited by AI systems than dense paragraph text.

Strategy 10: Build Your Presence on High-Authority Platforms (Reddit, Quora, etc.)

Your brand's perceived authority is not just determined by your website. Mentions on high-authority user-generated content platforms like Reddit and Quora, as well as industry-specific forums, can significantly increase the likelihood of being cited. When real people are referencing your brand as a solution, it sends a powerful signal to LLMs that your brand is a trusted entity in that domain.

High-Authority Platform Strategy:

  • Reddit: Participate in relevant subreddits with helpful content
  • Quora: Answer questions in your expertise area
  • Industry Forums: Engage in niche community discussions
  • LinkedIn: Share insights and engage with industry content
  • GitHub: For technical content, contribute to open-source projects
